# PlainCo Mission Statement
**Version:** 1.1  
**Date:** 2026-07-06  
**Status:** Active

## What We Build

Simple Android tools — one job per app, priced honestly, with no hidden agenda.

---

## What We Will Never Do

These are unconditional commitments. Not aspirations. Not policies subject to change when convenient.

1. **We will never show a blocking, interstitial, or session-interrupting ad.**  
   Free tiers may include a small static banner — honest, unobtrusive, removed permanently when you pay. What we will never do: full-screen ads, ads between actions, ads that interrupt what you are trying to do. If you paid, you never see an ad. That promise does not expire.

2. **We will never change the model of an app you paid for.**  
   No subscription introduced later. No feature moved from paid to subscription. No new category of data collection added to a paid app. No silent pivot of the app's purpose. The deal you bought under is the deal that applies, for as long as the app exists.

3. **We will never remove features from a paid version without offering a refund.**  
   If we deprecate something you paid for, we offer a refund or credit before the change takes effect — not after.

4. **We will never charge again for something you already own.**  
   A one-time purchase is a one-time purchase. A future subscription does not replace it, override it, or require you to re-buy what you have. What you paid for stays yours.

5. **We will never auto-enroll you in anything.**  
   No feature, no bundle, no subscription activates without your explicit action. Nothing is opt-out.

6. **We will never sell or share your data.**  
   Not to advertisers. Not to analytics platforms. Not to data brokers. Not to anyone. Our apps are built to not have data worth selling in the first place.

7. **We will never request unnecessary permissions.**  
   Every permission in every app manifest exists because the app cannot do its stated job without it. If the function doesn't require the permission, the permission is not there. You can verify this — the code is public.

8. **We will never abandon an app silently.**  
   If we stop supporting an app — for any reason — we will say so publicly and in advance, with a clear notice period. The first time you hear about it is not when the app stops working. Discontinuation is a public event, not a quiet one.

## What We Commit To

1. **Maintenance, honestly bounded.**  
   We will do our best to keep our apps compatible with current Android versions. We do not promise indefinite support. **You buy the current version. Future updates are not guaranteed.** The version you paid for keeps working on its own terms, even if we stop updating it. If we can no longer maintain an app, we will tell you before it stops working, not after.

2. **A free tier that is genuinely usable.**  
   When something is free in our apps, it works. Not crippled. Not time-limited. Not designed to frustrate you into paying. The free tier is the app. The paid unlock is more of the same app, for users who want it.

3. **Source code is public for all consumer apps.**  
   You can read it and audit it. Every claim we make is checkable.

4. **Bug reports are public and tracked.**  
   Every report submitted through the in-app feedback button creates a public GitHub issue. You can see the report. You can see when it is closed. You can see our explanation if we decide not to fix something. Nothing is handled silently.

5. **This mission statement changes publicly or not at all.**  
   If we ever need to change a commitment: we open a public issue explaining the proposed change, allow 30 days of community comment, and commit with a full explanation. If a change weakens a protection, affected users receive a refund on any active purchase before it takes effect. The full history of this document is visible in `git log`.

---

## If We Stop Supporting An App

This covers two cases: dropping support for an individual app while the company continues, and shutting the company down entirely. In both cases, the rule is the same: tell the public, in advance, with time to act.

- Public announcement of the change — with a clear reason and a clear date — published to the affected app's repo and to the org profile.
- Notice period of at least 90 days before the app stops working, more if we can.
- The last released version of the app continues to work indefinitely on devices that already have it installed. We do not push a remote update that breaks what you already paid for.
- All app code — including any remaining private portions — is open-sourced at announcement time, not at shutdown time. You can fork it, build it, and keep it running yourself.
- APK packages are published to GitHub Releases for sideloading, for users who want to continue using the app after Play Store removal.
- One-time purchase apps are published as fully offline builds. They do not depend on us, on a server, or on a Play Store listing, to keep working.

We designed for this from the start. The apps do not depend on us to keep running.
