## General practices — all PlainCo apps

### No accounts
No PlainCo app requires you to create an account or sign in.

### No analytics
No PlainCo app contains analytics tools, crash reporters, or background
telemetry of any kind. Nothing is collected or transmitted when you open
or use a PlainCo app.

### No ads
No PlainCo app contains advertising of any kind. No advertising SDKs are
present in any PlainCo app.

### Purchases
Where a PlainCo app offers an in-app purchase, it is processed entirely
by the platform's billing system (Google Play Billing on Android).
PlainCo never sees, receives, or stores your payment information.
Purchases are tied to your platform account and can be restored on any
device you sign into. See policies.google.com/privacy for how Google
handles billing data.

### Feedback
If you choose to submit feedback through any PlainCo app, your message
is posted as a public issue at github.com/PlainCo/.github/issues. Anyone
on the internet can read submitted feedback.

Before submitting you will be asked to confirm your message contains no
personal information. Do not include your name, email address, location,
or any other personal details in your feedback.

The following is always included: feedback category, app name, and your
message. If you check "Share device info", the following is also
included: app version, device model, OS version, screen resolution,
display theme, locale, available storage, and network type. This
checkbox is off by default. No device identifiers such as IMEI, device
ID, or serial number are ever included.

Submitting feedback is entirely optional.

### Children
No PlainCo app is directed at children under 13. PlainCo does not
knowingly collect data from children.

### Governing law
PlainCo is committed to protecting your privacy in accordance with the
General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable Canadian
privacy law. PlainCo does not maintain any database of user information.

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have additional
rights under GDPR including the right to access, correct, and request
deletion of your personal data. Contact plainco.apps@gmail.com to
exercise these rights.

## PlainQR — Scan & Generate

See the full PlainQR privacy policy at plainco.app/plainqr/privacy.

### Camera
PlainQR uses your camera to scan QR and barcode codes. Camera data is
processed entirely on your device and never transmitted, stored, or
shared.

### Photo library
PlainQR can scan QR codes from images you select from your gallery.
Images are processed locally and never transmitted or stored by PlainCo.

### Scan history
PlainQR saves a local history of scanned codes to a file on your device.
This file never leaves your device and is not accessible to PlainCo.

You can control this at any time:
- Turn off auto-saving in Settings
- Delete individual entries by swiping
- Delete all history with Clear All

WiFi passwords are never written to scan history — only the network name
(SSID) is saved. Passwords are displayed on screen, used to connect,
then discarded.

### WiFi
The WiFi QR generator reads your current network name (SSID) to
pre-fill the form. This happens on your device only and is not
transmitted anywhere.

When you scan a WiFi QR and tap Connect, PlainQR connects your device
to that network. Credentials are passed directly to Android's WiFi
system and not stored or transmitted by PlainCo.

### Location
The Location and Calendar QR generators can optionally access your GPS
coordinates to pre-fill a location field, only when you tap the location
button. Coordinates populate the form field and are immediately
discarded — never stored or transmitted by PlainCo.

On Android 12 and below, Android requires location permission to read
WiFi network names. PlainQR uses this solely to read your current SSID
for the WiFi generator — not to determine your physical location.

### Haptic feedback
PlainQR uses your device's vibration motor for haptic feedback on a
successful scan. No data is involved.
