# PlainCo Privacy Policy

*Last updated: July 15, 2026*

This policy applies to all apps published by PlainCo, including PlainQR — Scan & Generate. As new apps are released, they will be listed under [Apps covered by this policy](#apps-covered-by-this-policy) below.

PlainCo is an independent developer based in Ontario, Canada. You can reach us at plainco.apps@gmail.com.

## The short version

PlainCo apps do not collect, transmit, or sell your personal data. No account is required to use any PlainCo app. No analytics or tracking runs in the background. The only data that ever leaves your device is feedback you explicitly choose to submit.

## General practices — all PlainCo apps

### No accounts

No PlainCo app requires you to create an account or sign in to use it.

### No analytics

No PlainCo app contains analytics tools, crash reporters, or background telemetry of any kind. No data is collected or transmitted when you open or use a PlainCo app.

### No ads

No PlainCo app contains advertising of any kind. No advertising SDKs are present in any PlainCo app.

### Purchases

Where a PlainCo app offers an in-app purchase, it is processed entirely by the platform's billing system (Google Play Billing on Android). PlainCo never sees, receives, or stores your payment information. Purchases are tied to your platform account and can be restored on any device you sign into.

For information on how Google handles payment and billing data, refer to Google's Privacy Policy at policies.google.com/privacy.

### Feedback

If you choose to submit feedback through any PlainCo app, your message is posted as a public issue on PlainCo's GitHub repository at github.com/PlainCo/.github/issues. Anyone on the internet can read submitted feedback.

Before submitting, you will be asked to confirm that your message does not contain personal information. Do not include your name, email address, location, or any other personal details in your feedback message.

The following is always included in a feedback submission: feedback category, app name, and your message. If you check "Share device info" before submitting, the following is also included: app version, device model, OS version, screen resolution, display theme, locale, available storage, and network type. This checkbox is off by default. No device identifiers such as IMEI, device ID, or serial number are ever included.

Submitting feedback is entirely optional.

### Children

No PlainCo app is directed at children under the age of 13. PlainCo does not knowingly collect data from children.

### Governing law

PlainCo is committed to protecting your privacy in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable Canadian privacy law. PlainCo does not maintain any database of user information. If you submit feedback, it is posted publicly to github.com/PlainCo/.github/issues and contains only what you choose to write.

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have additional rights under GDPR including the right to access, correct, and request deletion of your personal data. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at plainco.apps@gmail.com.

## PlainQR — Scan & Generate

### Camera

PlainQR uses your camera to scan QR and barcode codes. Camera data is processed entirely on your device in real time and is never transmitted, stored, or shared.

### Photo library

PlainQR can scan QR codes from images you select from your gallery. Selected images are processed locally on your device and are never transmitted or stored by PlainCo.

### Scan history

PlainQR saves a local history of your scanned codes to a file on your device. This file never leaves your device and is not accessible to PlainCo.

You can control this feature at any time:
- Turn off auto-saving in Settings
- Delete individual entries by swiping them away
- Delete all history using the Clear All option

**WiFi scans and passwords:** When you scan a WiFi QR code, the network name (SSID) is saved to your history if auto-save is on. WiFi passwords are never written to your history file — they are displayed on screen and used to connect, then discarded.

### WiFi

The WiFi QR generator reads your current network name (SSID) to pre-fill the generator form. This happens entirely on your device and is not transmitted anywhere.

When you scan a WiFi QR code and tap Connect, PlainQR connects your device to the network using the credentials encoded in the QR code. These credentials are passed directly to Android's WiFi system and are not stored or transmitted by PlainCo.

### Location

The Location QR generator and Calendar QR generator can optionally access your device's GPS coordinates to pre-fill a location field. This is triggered only when you tap the location button. Coordinates are used to populate the form field and are discarded immediately — they are never stored to disk or transmitted by PlainCo.

On Android 12 and below, Android requires location permission to access WiFi network names. PlainQR uses this platform requirement solely to read your current SSID for the WiFi generator form — not to determine your physical location.

### Haptic feedback

PlainQR uses your device's vibration motor to provide haptic feedback on a successful scan. No data is involved.
